Travel Advisory

Japan is considered very safe for travelers with low crime rates and excellent infrastructure. Normal precautions are sufficient.

📋 Entry Requirements

Visa-free entry for short-term tourism (up to 90 days) for many nationalities, including U.S. passport holders. Passport should be valid for the duration of stay; onward/return ticket may be required.

🏥 Health

  • No mandatory vaccinations for entry
  • Routine vaccinations recommended
  • Travel insurance advised

⚠ Seasonal Risks

  • Earthquakes possible year-round
  • Typhoons mainly Aug–Oct; low risk in November
  • Occasional heavy rain

🎎 Etiquette

  • Bow slightly when greeting or thanking
  • Speak quietly on trains and public transport
  • Follow posted rules about photography, especially at shrines and temples
  • Carry cash, as some places do not accept cards
  • Remove shoes when required in temples, homes, and some restaurants

💵 Tipping

Tipping is not customary in Japan and can be considered rude. Excellent service is included in the price.

🍜 Dining Customs

  • Say 'itadakimasu' before eating and 'gochisousama deshita' after finishing
  • Do not stick chopsticks upright in rice
  • Slurping noodles is acceptable and shows appreciation
  • Return trays and dishes in casual eateries when indicated

👔 Dress Code

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and shrines
  • Comfortable walking shoes are essential due to extensive walking
  • Neat, understated clothing is common in cities

👔 Clothing

☑ Lightweight waterproof jacket — Protects against occasional November rain while staying breathable
☑ Comfortable walking shoes — Long days on foot exploring neighborhoods and temples
☑ Warm layers (sweater or fleece) — Allows easy layering for cool mornings and evenings
☐ Scarf — Adds warmth and style without bulk
☐ Compact umbrella — Quick protection from sudden showers

📄 Documents

☑ Passport — Required for international travel and hotel check-in
☐ Printed flight and hotel confirmations — Backup in case of phone or connectivity issues
