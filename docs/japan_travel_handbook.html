Morning: Train to Nara

Transportation Tips

Japan Rail Pass

Purchase a 7-day Japan Rail Pass before traveling to Japan (approximately $300 per person). This will cover your travel between Tokyo and Kyoto, day trips to Nara and Hiroshima, and many local JR trains.

IC Cards

Get a PASMO or Suica card for easy access to subways, local buses, and even some convenience store purchases. You can load money onto these cards as needed.

Tokyo Subway

The subway system is extensive but well-marked in English. Use Google Maps or a similar app for transit directions.

Key Attractions

Tokyo

Meiji Shrine

A peaceful shrine dedicated to Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken, surrounded by a lush forest in the heart of Tokyo.

Hours: Sunrise to sunset, daily

Fee: Free admission to the main shrine

Sensō-ji Temple

Tokyo's oldest temple, founded in 628 AD. Walk through the famous Kaminarimon Gate and browse the traditional shops along Nakamise Street.

Hours: 6:00 AM to 5:00 PM (Main hall)

Fee: Free

Kyoto

Fushimi Inari Shrine

Famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates winding up the mountainside. Early morning visits avoid crowds.

Hours: Always open

Fee: Free

Tip: The full hike takes 2-3 hours, but even walking through the initial densely packed gates provides a great experience.

Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)

A stunning Zen temple covered in gold leaf, reflected in a serene pond surrounded by meticulous gardens.

Hours: 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM

Fee: ¥400

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

A magical pathway through towering bamboo, creating a unique atmosphere as sunlight filters through the green canopy.

Hours: Always open

Fee: Free

Tip: Visit early morning (before 8:00 AM) to avoid crowds and get better photos.

Nara

Nara Park & Deer

Home to over 1,000 free-roaming "sacred" deer that bow for crackers (available for purchase in the park).

Hours: Always open

Fee: Free (deer crackers cost ¥200)

Todai-ji Temple

Houses the Great Buddha (Daibutsu), one of Japan's largest bronze Buddha statues (15 meters tall), in the world's largest wooden building.

Hours: 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM (April-October)

Fee: ¥600

Isuien Garden

A hidden gem in Nara with beautiful traditional landscaping, ponds, and borrowed scenery of Todai-ji's pagoda in the distance.

Hours: 9:30 AM to 4:30 PM

Fee: ¥1,000

Special Note: Perfect proposal location with peaceful atmosphere and beautiful natural settings.

Hiroshima

Peace Memorial Park & Museum

A sobering but important memorial to the victims of the atomic bombing, promoting peace and nuclear disarmament.

Hours: 8:30 AM to 6:00 PM (March-July)

Fee: ¥200 (museum)

Miyajima Island & Itsukushima Shrine

Famous for its "floating" torii gate, one of Japan's most iconic views. The island is also home to friendly deer.

Hours: 6:30 AM to 6:00 PM (shrine)

Fee: ¥300 (shrine)

Note: Check tide times to see the gate "floating" at high tide.

Travel Tips

Money & Payments

Japan is still largely a cash-based society, especially in smaller establishments.

  • Bring cash and exchange some at the airport upon arrival
  • 7-Eleven ATMs reliably accept foreign cards
  • Major hotels, department stores, and restaurants accept credit cards
  • Carry a coin purse – you'll accumulate many coins

Etiquette

  • Bow slightly when greeting people
  • Remove shoes when entering traditional accommodations, some restaurants, and temples
  • Do not tip (it's considered rude)
  • Stand on the left side of escalators in Tokyo (right side in Osaka/Kyoto)
  • Avoid eating while walking
  • Keep voice down on public transportation
  • When paying at shops, place money in the tray provided, not directly in hands

Connectivity

  • Rent a pocket WiFi device or get a data SIM upon arrival at the airport
  • Download offline maps before your trip
  • Install a translation app like Google Translate
  • Consider the Japan Transit Planner app for navigating trains

Food & Dining

  • Say "Itadakimasu" before eating (similar to "bon appétit")
  • Look for restaurants with plastic food displays if you can't read Japanese
  • Try conveyor belt sushi for affordable sushi options
  • Many restaurants have a "otōshi" or "table charge" with a small appetizer
  • Slurping noodles is considered a compliment to the chef

Budget Saving Tips

  • Convenience stores (konbini) like 7-Eleven, Lawson, and FamilyMart have quality, affordable food
  • Look for lunch sets ("teishoku") for better value than dinner
  • Consider staying at a ryokan (traditional inn) for one night rather than your entire stay
  • Use local buses in Kyoto (¥230 flat fare or ¥500 day pass) for efficient sightseeing
  • Take advantage of tax-free shopping (look for the sign) for purchases over ¥5,000

Special Moment Planning

Isuien Garden in Nara - The Perfect Proposal Location

The serene Isuien Garden is an ideal location for your proposal, combining natural beauty, privacy, and Japanese cultural significance.

Why Isuien Garden?

  • Less crowded than major tourist spots
  • Beautiful traditional Japanese garden with ponds and bridges
  • Peaceful atmosphere with the distant view of Todai-ji's pagoda
  • Represents the harmony and beauty of Japanese aesthetics
  • Several secluded spots perfect for an intimate moment

Best Spots Within the Garden

Sansui-tei Teahouse Area: A beautiful wooden structure overlooking the pond with Mt. Wakakusa and Todai-ji in the background. This "borrowed scenery" concept is quintessentially Japanese.

Back Garden Bridge: A small arched bridge crossing over a narrow section of the pond, surrounded by maple trees. In April, you might catch early blooming flowers.

Timing

Visit in the late afternoon when the lighting is golden and tourist numbers decrease. The garden closes at 4:30 PM, so aim to arrive by 2:30-3:00 PM to have time to explore and find the perfect spot.

Practical Tips

  • Entry fee: ¥1,000 per person
  • Address: 74 Suimoncho, Nara, 630-8208
  • 10-minute walk from Kintetsu Nara Station
  • Consider having a small celebration afterward at one of the nearby traditional restaurants
  • Take some photos discreetly before proposing, or consider hiring a local photographer

Backup Proposal Locations

Miyajima Island - Itsukushima Shrine

At sunset, the "floating" torii gate creates a magical backdrop. Find a quiet spot on the beach away from the main crowd.

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove (Early Morning)

If you arrive very early (before 7:30 AM), you might have this magical bamboo pathway almost to yourself, creating a surreal, peaceful environment.

Philosopher's Path in Kyoto

This peaceful stone path alongside a canal lined with cherry trees is named for the philosopher Nishida Kitaro who used this path for daily meditation. In mid-April, you may catch late-blooming cherry blossoms.
